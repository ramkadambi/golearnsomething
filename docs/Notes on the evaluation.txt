Track 1: The In-Person Workshop (The Observational Rubric)
In a physical clubhouse, you cannot easily track a student's eye movements or reading speed. The assessment here must rely entirely on the instructor's observation of the final output.

Execution Flow (The 3-Hour Workshop):

The Brief & Build (120 Minutes): Students form their Tribe, pick their capabilities, and execute the hardware build (e.g., the Smart Plant Alert). The instructor (Mentor K) acts purely as a facilitator, not a lecturer.

The Pitch (40 Minutes): Teams stand up to present their artifact.

The Stealth Scoring (Background): While the students present, the instructor uses a simple tablet app to log scores on the 3-Dimensional Rubric (Artifact Execution, Real-World Synthesis, Q&A Resilience).

The Output: This observational data is synced to the central platform, generating the video portfolio and the "Confidence & Capability Matrix" for the parents to share.

Track 2: The Monthly Subscription (The Digital Telemetry Engine)
This is where your AI-driven moat is truly built. For the at-home assignments, the student interacts directly with the LearnSMTH digital platform, allowing you to capture high-resolution behavioral telemetry.

Execution Flow (The Monthly Digital Assignment):

Multi-Modal Delivery: The platform presents the monthly mission. The student chooses their starting point: the visual comic, the audio brief, the fast-paced video, or the raw schematic.

Event-Driven Telemetry: Every click, pause, and skip is tracked. If a student breezes through the video but spends 20 minutes repeatedly clicking the visual circuit diagram before taking the end-of-mission quiz, the system logs this preference.

Adaptive Routing: For the next month's assignment, the platform dynamically reorders the content, prioritizing the visual schematics first to match their proven learning pathway, instantly reducing cognitive friction.

Building the Architecture (The Technical Moat)
To handle the real-time data from the monthly subscriptions without lag, the backend architecture requires a robust, scalable foundation. Leveraging an event-driven setup using Kafka to stream this real-time clickstream data, processed by a solid backend like Java 17, and stored in flexible JSON document structures within MongoDB is an ideal way to dynamically map these cognitive profiles. Furthermore, leveraging AI-assisted development platforms like Cursor, paired with advanced LLMs, will drastically accelerate the coding of this telemetry engine and the generation of the adaptive mission pathways.
