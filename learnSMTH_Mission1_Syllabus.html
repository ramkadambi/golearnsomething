Mission 1: Water From Air

LearnSMTH Grade 10 Transformation Journey

The Real-World Problem

Chennai is facing water shortages despite having high humidity for much of the year. The challenge for our Grade 10 students: Can we design a system that captures drinking water from the air?


Students act as engineers, scientists, environmentalists, entrepreneurs, and city planners. They don't just study subjects; they use them to build a real Atmospheric Water Generator (AWG).

Science Integration

1. Chemical Reactions and Equations

Concept Physical vs. Chemical Changes, Oxidation, Corrosion.

Mission Application: Students debate if condensation is a chemical reaction (it's physical). They then evaluate what happens to the metal parts of the water collector in Chennai's humidity over a year, making rust and material selection highly relevant.

2. Acids and Bases

Concept pH Levels, Minerals, Water Quality.

Mission Application: Students measure the pH of tap, rain, distilled, and packaged water to answer: "If we collect water from the air, can we drink it immediately?" They explore why minerals must be added for safe long-term drinking.

3. Carbon and Its Compounds

Concept Polymers, Food-grade Plastics, UV Resistance.

Mission Application: Why not use any plastic bottle? Students compare PET, HDPE, PVC, and Stainless Steel to select the best, safest material for storing atmospheric water.

4. Sources of Energy

Concept Renewable vs. Non-Renewable Energy.

Mission Application: An AWG runs all day. Teams calculate daily energy needs and compare Grid, Solar, Battery, and Wind power to propose the most suitable option for Chennai.

5. Our Environment & Life Processes

Concept Ecosystems, Digestion, Blood Circulation.

Mission Application: Students link local environment data (rainfall, groundwater depletion) with the biological necessity of clean water for kidneys, digestion, and life processes.

Mathematics Integration

1. Real Numbers & Linear Equations

Concept Estimation, Exact Values, Linear Variables.

Mission Application: A family uses 540L/day; an AWG produces 18L/day. How many machines are needed? Students create linear equations (e.g., 20x = daily requirement) to plan scaling and adjust for winter production drops.

2. Statistics & Probability

Concept Mean, Median, Variation, Predictive Probability.

Mission Application: Analyzing Chennai's humidity data to calculate the best week for production. Using weather forecasts to predict the probability of hitting maximum water yield.

3. Surface Area & Coordinate Geometry

Concept Area Calculations, Spatial Layout.

Mission Application: Redesigning the condensation collector's surface area for maximum yield. Using coordinate geometry to map an apartment rooftop for optimal AWG placement (considering sunlight and airflow).

Tech, Business & Humanities

English & Communication

Instead of writing essays, students act as consultants. They draft a Proposal to the Apartment Association, practicing persuasive writing, formal letters, and presentation skills to convince residents to install the AWG.

Social Science

Students compare the water shortages of Chennai with the extreme rainfall of Mawsynram. They investigate urbanization, public policy, and groundwater management.

Computer Science (AI)

Students design the logic algorithms for automation (e.g., IF humidity > 80% -> Turn Fan ON -> Collect Water -> Tank Full? -> Stop) before they even start coding.

Entrepreneurship

Teams run their project like a startup. They define customer segments, calculate pricing models, and plan maintenance and scaling strategies.
