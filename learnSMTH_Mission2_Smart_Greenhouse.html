Mission 2: Smart Greenhouse

LearnSMTH Grade 10 Transformation Journey

Engineering Theme: Sense → Decide → Act

The Real-World Problem

Year 2035: Food prices in Chennai have doubled. Erratic rainfall and severe heat waves are severely affecting vegetable production.


A startup wants to build smart greenhouses that automatically monitor plants and make intelligent decisions to improve growth while drastically reducing water usage. The challenge for our Grade 10 students: Can you build a greenhouse that thinks?


Building upon the observation skills from Mission 1, Mission 2 advances students into intelligent control and automation using soil moisture sensors, temperature sensors (DHT11), LDRs, water pumps, and grow lights.

Science Integration

1. Chemical Reactions & Periodic Classification

Concept Dissolution, Plant Nutrients, Elements (N, P, K).

Mission Application: Students compare dry fertilizers with water-mixed ones to understand physical dissolution versus chemical reactions. They investigate why elements like Nitrogen, Phosphorus, and Potassium are critical to crop yield.

2. Acids, Bases and Salts

Concept Soil pH.

Mission Application: Students test the pH of acidic, neutral, and alkaline soils. They must determine the optimum pH for growing specific crops like tomatoes, turning pH into an essential engineering design parameter.

3. Metals, Non-metals & Carbon Compounds

Concept Corrosion Resistance, Polymers, UV Stability.

Mission Application: Greenhouses are highly humid. Students compare iron, aluminum, stainless steel, and PVC frames to observe rusting. They also evaluate polymers (plastic, glass, polycarbonate) to select the safest, most durable, and UV-resistant covering material.

4. Life Processes, Control & Coordination

Concept Photosynthesis, Root Absorption, Plant Hormones.

Mission Application: Students link real-time sensor values directly to biology. They conceptually compare how plant hormones and the human nervous system make decisions to how their Arduino logic board controls the greenhouse environment.

5. Light & The Human Eye

Concept LED Spectrums, Reflection, Light Sensors.

Mission Application: Can red and blue LEDs help plants grow faster? Students investigate light intensity and compare how the human eye, a camera, and their LDR (light-dependent resistor) detect light.

6. Electricity & Magnetic Effects

Concept Voltage, Relays, Electromagnetism.

Mission Application: This forms the core electronics of the mission. Students learn about voltage and current by wiring water pumps and batteries. They open relay modules to observe how electromagnets function as automated switches.

Mathematics Integration

1. Real Numbers & Arithmetic Progression

Concept Tank Capacity, Growth Sequences.

Mission Application: Students calculate greenhouse volume and water tank capacities. They track plant growth over time (e.g., 2cm, 4cm, 6cm) to recognize arithmetic sequences and predict future height.

2. Linear & Quadratic Equations

Concept Water Planning, Light Intensity.

Mission Application: Students use linear equations to scale water requirements (if 1 plant needs 500ml, how much for 100 plants, factoring in rainfall?). They use quadratics to understand how light intensity diminishes with distance from the grow light.

3. Triangles, Circles & Trigonometry

Concept Roof Trusses, Sprinkler Coverage, Angles.

Mission Application: Students build paper models of roof trusses to load-test them, exploring why triangles are strong. They optimize circular sprinkler overlap to ensure no dry spots, and use trigonometry to calculate the ideal roof slope and solar panel inclination for Chennai's latitude.

4. Coordinate Geometry & Statistics

Concept Layout Mapping, Data Trends.

Mission Application: Students map the greenhouse layout on a grid to determine the shortest, safest wiring paths. They collect daily temperature, humidity, and light data, computing the mean and median to identify climate trends inside the dome.

Tech, Business & Humanities

English & Communication

Students step into professional roles to write comprehensive Instruction Manuals and Maintenance Guides for their smart greenhouses. They also draft video presentations and investor pitches.

Social Science

Students study the history of the Green Revolution and compare it to modern agricultural economics. They calculate the Return on Investment (ROI) for smart farming and investigate government subsidies available for tech adoption in agriculture.

Computer Science (AI & Automation)

Students program the actual control logic: IF Soil Moisture < 30% -> Turn Pump ON. IF Temperature > 35°C -> Turn Fan ON. They extend this to data logging and simple predictive models ("Water likely needed tomorrow").

Entrepreneurship

Teams found GreenGrow Technologies. They define their customer segments (urban farms, apartment complexes, schools), establish a recurring revenue model for monitoring, and pitch their competitive advantage to investors.

"By the end of Mission 2, students are no longer just building projects—they are thinking like systems engineers. Every NCERT subject is required to make their greenhouse intelligent."
